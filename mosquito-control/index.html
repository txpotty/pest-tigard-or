Understanding the Mosquito Life Cycle

Mosquitoes go through four distinct life stages: egg, larva, pupa, and adult. The first three stages occur in water. A female mosquito lays her eggs in or near standing water. These eggs hatch into larvae, which live in the water and feed on organic matter. The larvae then develop into pupae, a non-feeding stage before emerging as adult mosquitoes. This understanding highlights the importance of targeting water sources in any control strategy.

Knowing that mosquitoes rely on water for most of their life cycle makes eliminating standing water the most crucial preventative step you can take. If you remove the water source, you break the cycle and prevent new mosquitoes from developing. This is why our inspections heavily focus on identifying and advising on how to eliminate standing water sources, helping homeowners become active participants in their own mosquito control efforts. It's a simple yet incredibly effective strategy that complements our professional treatments.
